Understanding anti-wrinkle injections

Anti-wrinkle injections use Botulinum Toxin Type A to temporarily relax targeted muscles. The treatment works by reducing muscle movement, helping to soften existing lines and prevent deeper wrinkle formation.

Common treatment areas include: forehead lines, frown lines (glabella), crow's feet, masseter (jaw slimming), bruxism (teeth grinding), Nefertiti lift (neck & jawline) and hyperhidrosis (excess sweating).

  • Results usually begin to appear within 2–10 days.
  • Full results are generally visible after approximately 14 days.
  • Results typically last around 3–4 months but vary between individuals.

Risks & potential side effects

I understand that possible side effects include:

Common

  • Redness
  • Swelling
  • Tenderness
  • Bruising
  • Mild headache
  • Temporary heaviness
  • Injection-site discomfort

Less common

  • Eyelid drooping (ptosis)
  • Brow heaviness
  • Uneven results
  • Double vision
  • Dry eyes
  • Excess tearing
  • Facial asymmetry
  • Difficulty with certain expressions

Rare

  • Allergic reaction
  • Infection
  • Muscle weakness outside the intended area
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Difficulty speaking
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Anaphylaxis

I understand that, although uncommon, serious complications may require urgent medical treatment.
