The first 4 hours

Please do

  • Remain upright
  • Carry on with normal light daily activities
  • Gently use the treated muscles if advised — frowning, raising your eyebrows, smiling

Please avoid

  • Lying down or napping
  • Bending forward for prolonged periods
  • Touching, rubbing or massaging the area
  • Tight hats or headwear
  • Applying makeup to the injection points
Why? The product needs time to bind to the intended muscles. Staying upright and avoiding pressure helps minimise the already small risk of unwanted product migration.

The first 24–48 hours

Please avoid

  • Gym, running, weightlifting & high-intensity exercise
  • Saunas, steam rooms, hot baths & sunbeds
  • Facial treatments, facial massage, microneedling & chemical peels
  • Excessive alcohol
Why? Heat and increased circulation may increase bruising and can affect how the treatment settles.

What is completely normal?

Common & expected

  • Small bumps at injection sites
  • Mild redness
  • Mild bruising
  • Mild tenderness
  • Mild headache
  • Tightness or heaviness
  • A strange sensation when moving the muscles
  • Some movement still being present

Most of these settle naturally within a few days.

Please don't judge your result too early

One of the most common concerns we hear is "it's not working." Anti-wrinkle injections do not work immediately — they develop gradually:

Days 2–5

Early softening of muscle movement.

Days 5–10

Increasing relaxation.

Around day 14

Final, settled result.

Your treatment should not be assessed before approximately 2 weeks.

Why can I still move?

This is completely normal. Anti-wrinkle injections aim to soften muscle activity, not necessarily freeze all movement. Many clients keep:

Perfectly normal to keep

  • Natural facial expression
  • Some movement
  • Character & facial animation

…while still enjoying smoother skin. Movement during the first week does not mean the treatment has failed.

Understanding heaviness

Some clients experience forehead or brow heaviness, tightness, or a sense that the forehead feels different. This is usually temporary while your muscles adjust to their new movement patterns, and in most cases settles naturally over the first few weeks.

Understanding asymmetry

As the treatment develops, one side may occasionally respond slightly faster than the other, which can create temporary asymmetry during the first 2 weeks.

Do not judge symmetry before day 14. A proper assessment can only be made once the treatment has fully developed — and any small adjustment can be made at your review.

What is NOT normal — contact us

Please contact Layali Clinic if you experience:
  • Increasing redness, swelling or heat
  • Pus or discharge, or fever
  • Significant pain
  • Marked facial weakness or severe asymmetry
  • Drooping eyelid (ptosis) or drooping eyebrow
  • Double vision
  • Difficulty swallowing, speaking or breathing
Difficulty swallowing, speaking or breathing is extremely rare and requires urgent medical attention. Contact Layali Clinic on 07787 708200; if you can't reach us, seek urgent medical care immediately.

A final reassurance

The first few days are not your final result. The treatment develops gradually, and a little patience is important. Most clients enjoy a smooth recovery with little or no downtime, and a softer, fresher, more relaxed appearance once it has fully settled.
